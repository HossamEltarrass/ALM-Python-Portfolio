## Overview

This is the **capstone project** of a 12-project ALM Python portfolio built from scratch.

It consolidates every verified headline result from Projects 1–11 into a single **ALCO-level Excel dashboard** — the format used by Treasury and ALM desks at major banks to present risk metrics to the Asset & Liability Committee (ALCO).

The entire dashboard is generated by Python via **xlwings** (live Python ↔ Excel connection), producing a fully formatted, multi-sheet workbook with:
- RAG (Red / Amber / Green) traffic light status on every metric
- Regulatory thresholds shown alongside current values
- Embedded matplotlib charts on all 6 sheets
- Major European G-SIB institutional colour scheme throughout
- **One-click Refresh button** — update all data without touching the notebook

---

## What is ALCO?

The **Asset & Liability Committee** is the senior risk committee at every major bank. It meets monthly and reviews all balance sheet risks — interest rate risk, liquidity, capital, and market risk — against regulatory limits and internal thresholds. The ALCO pack is the single document the CFO, CRO, and Treasurer read before every meeting.

This dashboard replicates that format exactly.

## Key Findings

**Interest Rate Risk**
The bank is asset-sensitive — rate hikes boost NII (+€1,591m at +200bps) but Rate Cut is the worst NII scenario (−€5,893m). Net EVE/T1 of 13.24% sits just inside the 15% outlier threshold thanks to the 55% IRS overlay; without it, gross EVE would be 29.4% of Tier 1 — a serious regulatory breach.

**Liquidity**
LCR passes all 4 scenarios comfortably (min 152.9%). NSFR is the binding constraint — the insurance book (€305bn at 100% RSF) creates structural funding pressure. Stress NSFR breaches 100% at 99.8%. Stress survival horizon is only 2 months — satisfies Basel LCR (30 days) but is close to the internal 3M ILAAP escalation threshold.

**Capital**
Bank is self-capitalising — CET1 drifts from 15.16% to 15.76% over 4 years. The CET1 paradox: under ICAAP severe stress, CET1 actually improves to 16.49% because loan contraction shrinks RWA faster than equity falls. RWA treadmill consumes 78.6% of retained earnings — only 21.4% is true organic buffer build.

**Market Risk**
Portfolio DV01 of €100.88m/bp. ES Bono 30Y is the single largest risk contributor (29.0% of total DV01). Convexity benefit at +200bps saves €2,276m vs the linear duration estimate. Yield curve is hump-shaped — peaks at 7Y (2.860%), slight inversion from 5Y to 10Y.

**FTP & Behavioural**
Deposit desk drives 85.1% of total FTP NII (€20,395m) on franchise margin of 1.85%. Mortgage desk contributes €3,580m on NIM of 1.29%. CPR Rate Cut is the worst behavioural scenario (−€2,808m NII) as prepayment wave forces reinvestment at lower yields.

## One-Click Refresh

The dashboard includes a **"Refresh Dashboard"** button embedded directly on the ALCO Scorecard sheet.

**How to use it:**
1. Edit `data.py` — update any number (e.g. change a rate, balance, or ratio)
2. Open `ALM_Dashboard.xlsx`
3. Click the green **"Refresh Dashboard"** button (top-right of Sheet 1)
4. Excel rebuilds all 6 sheets and 6 charts automatically in ~30 seconds

**How it works:**
The button triggers a VBA macro that calls `refresh.py` via the Python virtual environment. `refresh.py` reads the updated `data.py` and rewrites the entire workbook. No manual re-running of the notebook required.

> **Note:** The file must be saved as `.xlsm` (macro-enabled workbook) to support the button. Trust Center → Macro Settings → Enable macros must be on.
